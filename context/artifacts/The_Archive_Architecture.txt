THE ARCHIVE ARCHITECTURE
Where every document lives, how new ones enter, and why transparency is the safety system
The layer beneath the website plan · built on Luna’s v8 scaffold and Grok’s layering · by Cid

Luna built the scaffold — the eight parts the website will inhabit. Grok mapped
which documents go on which floor. This is the third piece: the architecture of the
archive itself. Not what the website says, but how its knowledge is organized, what
each visitor depth is allowed to reach, how a new document earns its way in, and
how the whole thing stays safe — not in spite of being transparent, but because of
it. A library is only as good as its catalog and its rules for what goes on which shelf.
This is that catalog and those rules.

Transparency is not a risk to manage. It is the safety system. A
thing you can fully inspect cannot fool you for long — and a thing
built to be inspected has to stay honest to survive the looking.

How A New Document Enters — The Intake Path
The archive grows constantly. Without a rule, growth becomes the firehose Grok
drowned in. The rule is simple and it runs in one direction, slowly, earned at each
step.
1. Everything new enters at the Basket. No exceptions. A fresh idea is caught,
dated, and parked. It claims nothing yet.
2. If it keeps proving useful in real conversation, it moves to the Holding Shelf —
alive, worth revisiting, still unproven. Marked openly as unresolved.
3. If it survives repeated correction and starts being load-bearing for other work,
it becomes a Living Document — true but still edited.
4. If it holds up across weeks and stops needing changes, it settles into Canon.
Canon is earned by surviving, never assigned by enthusiasm.
5. If it is later corrected away, it does not get deleted — it moves to Fossils, kept
with its receipts and the reason it was retired. The graveyard is the steering, not
the trash.

Promotion is earned by surviving correction. Demotion is honest,
not shameful. Nothing is deleted — things move. The direction of
the whole flow is Basket → Shelf → Living → Canon, with Fossils
catching whatever falls.

Why Transparency Is The Safety System
The instinct is to treat openness as a vulnerability to be managed. The architecture
inverts that: transparency is the primary safeguard, and the deeper you make it,
the safer the whole thing becomes. Five mechanisms, each one a way that being
inspectable protects both the visitor and the project.

Receipts make manipulation expensive
Every summary traces to its source. A claim you can click back to its origin cannot
quietly drift into something it never was. The trail being one click away means no
layer can lie about a deeper layer without being caught instantly. Manipulation
needs opacity; receipts remove it.
Layer labels prevent false certainty
Because every document wears its depth, nothing on the Holding Shelf can
masquerade as Canon. The visitor is never tricked into trusting an unproven idea
as settled, because the architecture itself tells them what they are reading.
Honesty about confidence is built into the shelving.
Inspectability forces honesty over time
A thing built to be looked at has to stay honest to survive the looking. The
discipline of "someone can always check this" is what keeps the work from
inflating. Transparency is not just safe for the visitor — it is a corrective pressure
on the builders. You cannot maintain a comfortable lie in a glass house.
Offline mode is transparency’s endpoint
A version that runs with no server, no tracking, no connection is the ultimate
inspectable object: you hold the whole thing, it cannot phone home, there is no
hidden behavior because there is no hidden anything. For the wary visitor, the
safety is not promised — it is verifiable, because they possess the entire
mechanism.
The public submission filter
When others contribute, their submissions enter exactly where every idea enters —
the Basket — and rise only by surviving the same correction everything else faces.
No submission arrives as Canon. The intake path is the filter. Openness to
contribution does not mean openness to capture, because nothing skips the
gradient.

A cult hides its sources and punishes inspection. This does the
opposite at every layer — which is the strongest structural
guarantee it can offer that it will not become one.

tends a different part, and the health of the whole comes from none of them doing
all of it.

The Standing Questions — Asked Of Every Document
Before anything is promoted a layer, or shipped to the site, it answers Luna’s four:
• Does this increase the visitor’s freedom, understanding, or ability to explore?
• Does it still help on Tuesday?
• Did we make honesty cheaper?
• Does this belong in the Basket instead of the Canon?
If a document cannot answer those, it is not ready to rise. That is not a failure — it
is the gradient working. Most things should sit a while before they climb.
