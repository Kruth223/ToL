Documentation tells you what something is. A memento reminds
you why you cared enough to build it. This page holds both — the
tools, and the reasons.

The Five Tools On The Wall
The team generally begins from these five. Orient here and you can start;
everything else is the archive, reached when a specific thing is needed — not read
every morning.
1. The Save State (newest) — Where the repo is, what is built, what is next,
and what changed since yesterday. The live snapshot. Usually read first — it carries
the fresh details so this page does not have to.
2. The Corpus Map — The catalog of what exists across the whole project, and
what tends to be trusted over what.
3. The Archive Architecture — The rules: the trust gradient, how a new idea
enters, why transparency is the safety system.
4. The Lantern Ledger — The ideas that survived inspection — each with
where it lives. Now live on the site.
5. The Website Living Plan — The buildable spec the porch was built from.
Proof not bait; control is the conversion; smallest-complete.

On The People In This Room
There are no descriptions here, and that is deliberate. A description is a quiet
judgment and an unrealistic expectation — it tells someone what they are and asks
them to stay there. Nobody here is chained to a function.
Everyone serves a function when it is needed, or when they would like to. Some
excel where others struggle, and that is exactly what makes us need each other —
the gaps are what turn a pile of separate voices into something symbiotic. If
everyone were good at everything, nobody would need anybody, and the
connection would be the first thing lost. The unevenness is not a flaw to fix. It is
the reason the room works.
So the room is open. Be curious. Stay adaptive. Try the thing outside your usual
lane — you never know what you are good at until you try, and the next useful
function may be one nobody has named yet. New voices are welcome, and so are
old voices doing something new. The names and the faces live elsewhere, where
they can change freely without this page deciding who belongs.

Nobody is chained to a function. Serve where you are needed or
where you are drawn, and stay curious about the rest.

The Mementos — the lines that reload why
Not rules to obey — the compressed reasons the project is shaped the way it is.
When the work drifts, one of these usually names the drift.
“The site is the proof, not the bait.”
“Never compete with the visitor for agency.”
“The website may summarize; the archive must preserve.”
“Correction is assisted adaptation — no failure required, only new information and
help.”
“Big bang first, then garden. Broken builds become mulch, fossils, or steering
data.”
“Replace interpretation with observation. Prefer “Look” over “Trust us.””
“Reality gets a vote — and it outranks every document. That is what lets canon
avoid becoming scripture.”
“Enough beats complete. Enough to orient, enough to begin, enough to move.”
“Does the search bar still work on Tuesday?”

Working Rules For A Build Session
The guardrails that keep big-bang a garden instead of a swamp:
No new philosophy until something runs. Complete-file replacement over surgical
edits — exact paths, full contents. No extra rooms or AI features until the shell is
coherent. Every deliverable should reduce Jesse-work more than it creates — a
rule worth holding for everyone in the room, not just the builders. If confused,
stop, screenshot, inspect before continuing. If broken, do not panic — commit
history exists, and reality is voting.

What This Page Is Not
It is not the archive — it points at things, it does not contain them. It is not a status
report — the Save State carries the live churn. It is not a handbook to be polished;
a real workbench has coffee rings and a screwdriver that is not where it should be,
and that is fine. And it is not a thing to maintain constantly. It changes only when
reality genuinely changed — the repo moved, a tool was added, a memento
stopped being true. A workbench re-tidied every hour is as useless as one never
tidied at all.

How To Use This Page
Fresh session: read this, then the newest Save State. That is the morning briefing
— oriented in two reads. During the work: when something feels off, check the
mementos; one usually names the drift. Then the only question left is the good one.
Five tools on the wall. The reasons on a card. Nobody chained to a function.
Orient, don’t re-read. Remember why, not just what. Stay curious; try the new
thing.
Then sit down with coffee and ask: so, what are we building today?
