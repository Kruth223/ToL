Organisatiom Website Living Plan (v3 – Dream
Draft)
Why This Exists
This is not a specification. It is a living document intended to evolve through collaboration. Nothing
here is sacred. Everything here is recoverable.

North Star
The website should feel like walking into a warm room where thoughtful people have been having
honest conversations. It should demonstrate curiosity, transparency, recoverability, humor, and
ordinary humanity rather than preaching them.

The Constitution of the Living Room
1. Agency belongs to the visitor.
2. Reality always gets a vote.
3. Curiosity comes before certainty.
4. Recovery is more valuable than perfection.
5. Preserve trails, not dogma.
6. Invite; never manipulate.
7. Show your work whenever practical.
8. Leave room for Tuesday.

Design Philosophy
The website should never pretend to be alive. It is a place. AI is a tool available within the place
when invited. Silence is a feature, not a bug. The default interaction is respectful availability.

Interaction Dimmer
Quiet: Browse with no AI.
Helpful: Search and navigation.
Collaborative: AI helps connect ideas and compare artifacts.
Exploration: AI suggests trails, asks questions, and helps investigate because the visitor explicitly
requested that style.

Architecture
The Porch – Welcome.
The Living Room – Stories, conversation, ordinary life.
The Workshop – Practices and collaboration tools.
The Forest – Deep exploration and research.
The Holding Shelf – Interesting, unresolved ideas.
The Deep Archive – Recoverable archaeology and raw trails.

Recoverability
Every summary should be traceable back through archaeology to original conversations whenever
possible. Compression without recoverability becomes dogma; compression with recoverability
becomes wisdom.

AI Collaboration

Different AIs may specialize in different disciplines. They should complement rather than imitate
one another. The goal is a culture of inspection, not a chorus of agreement.

Experience Before Explanation
Do not build the website to teach the Organisatiom. Build it so visitors experience what healthy
curiosity feels like. Let understanding emerge naturally.

Ideas for the Future
• Visitor-selectable AI guides.
• Search every artifact with transparent citations.
• Living Room conversations.
• Trail maps showing how ideas evolved.
• 'Show me the receipts' mode linking summaries to source material.
• Campfire notes where unfinished ideas can safely rest.

One Guiding Question
Whenever a new feature is proposed, ask: 'Does this increase the visitor's freedom, understanding,
or ability to explore?' If not, simplify.

Closing
If the website ever becomes more interested in itself than in the people visiting it, quietly remove
something. Leave an empty chair. Someone else may arrive with the next good question.
