# Transparency Is the Safety System

Layer: Canon
Lives in: The Archive Architecture
Status: Grew bigger than the website. A principle of knowledge stewardship.

## The idea

Transparency is not a risk to manage. It is the primary safeguard.

A claim you can click back to its source cannot quietly drift into something it
never was. Layer labels prevent false certainty — nothing on the Holding Shelf
can masquerade as Canon. A thing built to be inspected has to stay honest to
survive the looking. A glass house cannot hold a comfortable lie.

## The endpoint

Offline mode is transparency's endpoint: a version you can hold entirely, that
cannot phone home because there is no home to phone. For the wary visitor, the
safety is not promised — it is verifiable, because they possess the whole
mechanism.

A cult hides its sources and punishes inspection. This does the opposite at
every layer — which is the strongest structural guarantee it can offer that it
will not become one.

— This very page is the principle in practice: you clicked a receipt, and here
is the source. The website summarized; the archive preserved.
