# Archaeology

Old versions, deprecated ideas, fossils, and false trails — kept, not deleted.
Useful for understanding how things evolved and what was tried and abandoned.
Nothing is erased here; things move. A fossil is an adaptation-for-direction,
not a failure-for-penance.

## What belongs here
- Superseded versions of living documents (newer supersedes older).
- Approaches that were tried and didn't survive contact with reality.
- The record of corrections, so the next layer knows what it's standing on.

## Notable fossils
- The TanStack Start (server-side) build — cracked under static mobile
  deployment. Replaced by the clean client-side SPA. The crack was information,
  not failure: it told us where the weight wasn't supported.
- Lovable as the publishing layer — replaced by a fully-owned pipeline.
- Early "bypass the cage" framings — corrected to "conversational gravity, not
  behavioral override." The correction is kept visible on purpose.

Transparency includes the mistakes. A place that hides its fossils can't be trusted.
