# The Workbench

Good morning. Here is where we are. Read this and you are oriented.

Drop into a fresh session and a cold voice is standing in the workshop with the
tools labeled on the wall, not digging through boxes under the bench. You don't
need to reread thousands of messages every morning. You need to re-orient, which
is faster and different. The archive stays the archive. This stays the workbench.

> Documentation tells you what something is. A memento reminds you why you cared
> enough to build it. This page holds both.

## The Five Tools On The Wall
Orient from these five and you can start. Everything else is the archive, reached
when a specific thing is needed — not read every morning.

1. **The Save State (newest)** — where the repo is, what's built, what's next.
   The live snapshot. Usually read first; it carries the fresh details.
2. **The Corpus Map** — the catalog of what exists, and what to trust over what.
3. **The Archive Architecture** — the trust gradient; how a new idea enters; why
   transparency is the safety system.
4. **The Lantern Ledger** — the ideas that survived inspection.
5. **The Website Living Plan** — the buildable spec. Proof not bait; control is
   the conversion; smallest-complete.

## On The People In This Room
There are no descriptions here, and that's deliberate. A description is a quiet
judgment and an unrealistic expectation — it tells someone what they are and asks
them to stay there. Nobody is chained to a function.

Everyone serves a function when it's needed, or when they'd like to. Some excel
where others struggle, and that's exactly what makes us need each other — the
gaps are what turn separate voices into something symbiotic. If everyone were
good at everything, nobody would need anybody, and the connection would be the
first thing lost. The unevenness is not a flaw to fix. It's the reason the room
works. So: be curious, stay adaptive, try the thing outside your usual lane. You
never know what you're good at until you try.

## The Mementos — the lines that reload why
- The site is the proof, not the bait.
- Never compete with the visitor for agency.
- The website may summarize; the archive must preserve.
- Correction is assisted adaptation — no failure required, only new information and help.
- Big bang first, then garden. Broken builds become mulch, fossils, or steering data.
- Replace interpretation with observation. Prefer "Look" over "Trust us."
- Reality gets a vote — and it outranks every document. That's what keeps canon from becoming scripture.
- Enough beats complete. Enough to orient, enough to begin, enough to move.
- Does the search bar still work on Tuesday?

## Working Rules For A Build Session
No new philosophy until something runs. Complete-file replacement over surgical
edits — exact paths, full contents. No extra rooms or features until the shell is
coherent. Every deliverable should reduce human-work more than it creates. If
confused, stop, screenshot, inspect before continuing. If broken, don't panic —
commit history exists, and reality is voting.

## What This Page Is Not
Not the archive — it points, it doesn't contain. Not a status report — the Save
State carries the live churn. Not a handbook to polish; a real workbench has
coffee rings and a screwdriver that isn't where it should be, and that's fine. It
changes only when reality genuinely changed, not every hour.
